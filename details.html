About New Washington





At the onset of the Philippine Revolution against Spain in 1897, “Fonda Lagatic” was one of the strategic places in the Visayas where the Katipunan, lead by Andres Bonifacio, organized the local revolutionary movement. It gave birth to the historic “Pacto de Sangre,” a secret revolutionary pact by a handful but patriotic native young men in “Sitio Kuntang” Fonda Lagatic on March 3, 1897. The local “Katipuneros” incised their forearms and used their own blood to affix their signatures and to signal the start of the revolution in Aklan.
In April 4, 1903, when the Americans established their new colonial rule in the Philippines, the residents of Fonda Lagatic petitioned the Philippine Commission to create the settlements of “Batang, Jimeno” (present-day Altavas), Balete, and Fonda Lagatic into a new municipality to be named New Washington, in honor of the first President of the United States of America, George Washington. The seat of government was established in “Fonda Lagatic”. In January 1904, Mr. Juan Oquendo of Balete was elected as the Municipal President during New Washington’s first municipal elections. The newly-formed municipality was inaugurated and the first set of municipal officials was sworn into office.
The three settlements of “Batan”, “Jimeno”, and “Balete” later on asked for separation from New Washington to become new independent municipalities. The municipality was left with the main coastal lands, an islet just across the Lagatic River and the river tributaries, marshlands and forest mangroves in the coastal area, and plain farmlands in the interior frontier.
When Aklan separated from Capiz on April 25, 1956, New Washington became one of the seventeen (17) municipalities of the newly created province. Presently, New Washington has comprised of sixteen (16) barangays; eight (8) barangays in the coastal areas, and eight (8) in the farmlands. It ranks third among the most progressive municipalities in Aklan namely: Kalibo, Malay, New Washington, and Ibajay in terms of population, trade and commerce, tourism industry, infrastructure projects, and other major investment programs.
Since 1904, The Municipality of New Washington has elected eighteen (18) Municipal Mayors to be the LCE. Honorable Jessica Regenio-Panambo is the present Municipal Mayor. She was elected on May 9, 2022.



TOURISM INDUSTRY

The recent development changes have been noted in the investments profile of the municipality as investments in tourism industry has doubly increased, particularly in the Talabahan Seafood Village in Barangay Tambak. This notable growth in tourism-related establishments in Barangay Tambak is mainly due to the completion of an elevated seawall that provides protection to the residents, businesses and infrastructures in the area from the adverse sea actions from Sibuyan Sea. In tourism industry, New Washington has a great tourism potential. It has 14 kilometers long beach facing the Sibuyan Sea starting from Barangay Tambak where the Talabahan Village is booming and frequented by local and foreign tourists for its fresh sea foods and the after-sundown gigs.
